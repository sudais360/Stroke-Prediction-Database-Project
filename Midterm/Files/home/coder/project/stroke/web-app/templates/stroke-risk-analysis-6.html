Demographic Analysis of Stroke Prevalence

This analysis explores stroke prevalence across various demographics, focusing on age group, gender, and marital status:

Age Group and Stroke Prevalence

  • 18-34 Years: High stroke prevalence, indicating a need for investigation into underlying causes.
  • 35-49 Years: Slight increase in prevalence, aligning with the increased stroke risk with age.
  • 50-64 Years: Prevalence often exceeding 50%, consistent with general stroke risk understanding.
  • 65+ Years: Varied but generally around 50%, suggesting effective management in this age group.

Gender Differences

Similar stroke prevalence in males and females, with minor variations suggesting gender-specific risk factors.

Marital Status

Variation in stroke prevalence possibly indicating the influence of social and psychological factors related to marital status.

Overall Observations

Stroke is a significant concern across all age groups, including younger populations. The data underscores the importance of targeted health interventions and awareness tailored to specific demographic groups.
