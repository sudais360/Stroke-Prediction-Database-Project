Dietary Habits and Stroke Correlation Analysis

Our analysis of dietary habits among patients and their correlation with stroke incidents reveals the following:

  • Gluten-Free: 49.6% of gluten-free patients (1026 out of 2068) had a history of stroke.
  • Vegetarian: 48.9% of vegetarians (1062 out of 2170) experienced a stroke.
  • Non-Vegetarian: 49.6% of non-vegetarian patients (1063 out of 2144) had strokes.
  • Vegan: 49.9% of vegans (1090 out of 2185) had a history of stroke.
  • Keto: 50.8% of keto diet followers (1083 out of 2135) experienced a stroke.
  • Paleo: 51.0% of paleo diet patients (1118 out of 2192) had strokes.
  • Pescatarian: 48.7% of pescatarians (1026 out of 2106) had a history of stroke.

The analysis indicates that there is no significant variance in stroke prevalence among different dietary groups, with stroke rates ranging from approximately 48.7% to 51.0%. It is crucial to understand that this analysis does not imply causation. Further research is necessary to explore any potential causal relationships between specific dietary habits and the risk of stroke.
