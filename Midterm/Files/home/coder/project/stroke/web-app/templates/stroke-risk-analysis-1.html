Analysis Report: Stroke Rates in Different Patient Groups

Overview

This report presents an analysis of stroke rates in two distinct patient groups, categorized by their lifestyle choices.

Group Comparisons

  • Group 1 (UnHealthy): Currently Smokes, Frequent Drinker, Low Exercise
    • Patients: 195
    • Stroke Rate: 1.30%
  • Group 2 (Healthy): Non-smoker, Never Drink, High Exercise
    • Patients: 203
    • Stroke Rate: 1.35%

Findings

Group 2 (Healthy) exhibits a marginally higher stroke rate than Group 1 (UnHealthy), by 0.05 percentage points.

Insights

The impact of lifestyle on health is complex, involving factors beyond traditional lifestyle markers. A holistic health perspective is crucial.

Recommendations

Expand health education, encourage regular health check-ups, and promote comprehensive community health programs.

Conclusion

The slightly higher stroke rate in Group 2 suggests a need to reevaluate our understanding of 'healthy' lifestyle choices.
